The project

"Tunnelsyn" was a large-scale group project completed as part of my final bachelor’s exam in Media and Interaction Design. Tunnelsyn was one out of five intallments that was part of the exhibition "Ambivalent Intelligence". Tunnelsyn is an installation designed to immerse visitors in the world of news media shaped by AI. It featured multiple screens, each powered by a Raspberry Pi and an ultrasonic sensor. As visitors passed by, the sensors triggered the Raspberry Pi to change the content displayed on the screens, creating an interactive experience. In the group we divided the roles roughly between physical design and digital design, whereas I had the project lead.

The thought behind the exhibition is to showcase the many positive and negative sides with artificial intelligence, and its influence on our society. Tunnelsyn is in short terms an intallment meant to shed light on the up and coming image- and video generative AI tools. With this project we wanted to showcase both the fasciniating and problematic side of this technology, as it permeates our lives through all sorts of media.

As part of the build up to this projects we teamed up with an established project in the field, called "Reynir". Through Project Reynir we got insights in past, current and future issues in the field of image/video generetive AI in the media- and news field - especailly focusing on misinformation. Our installation wanted to showcase the issues we may encounter because of this new technology, but we also wanted to introduce some solutions — enter Reynir.
