The Project

“Cue” was a student group project carried out as part of the MIX202 course at the University of Bergen. The prototype aimed to create a tool that supports people who struggle with social interaction and daily tasks. It introduced multiple search methods—sketch, position, text, and voice—to ensure accessibility and universal design. The prototype also included mini-games that let users practice everyday social interactions. The design and UX were intentionally kept simple to serve people with disabilities or social anxiety.

The project was based on an assignment to explore video retrieval technology—a method for retrieving media, such as videos or images, from a predefined database. The technology relies on text search or speech-to-text. We wanted to apply this in a realistic scenario, which led to Cue—an interactive tool for practicing social signals.
