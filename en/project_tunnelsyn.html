The Project

The large-scale project “Tunnelsyn” was completed as part of my final B.A. exam in Media & Interaction Design. It was one of five installations in the exhibition “Ambivalent Intelligence”. Tunnelsyn lets visitors experience news media shaped by AI. Several screens—each powered by a Raspberry Pi and an ultrasonic sensor—reacted as visitors walked by, changing the content to create an interactive experience. Within the team we split the work between physical and digital design; I was project lead.

Our goal was to highlight both the positive and negative sides of artificial intelligence and its impact on society. In short, Tunnelsyn focused on emerging image- and video-generative AI tools. We wanted to show both the fascinating and the problematic aspects as this technology permeates more and more media forms.

Before the build we collaborated with “Reynir”, an established project in the field. Through Reynir we gained insight into past, current, and future challenges related to generative AI in news and media—especially misinformation. Our installation aimed to surface these issues and point to potential mitigations—enter Reynir.
